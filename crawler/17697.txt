John Burke Krasinski (born October 20, 1979) is an American actor, screenwriter, producer, and director. He is most widely known for his role as Jim Halpert on the NBC sitcom The Office (2005¨C2013), for which he received critical acclaim and won a number of awards. He also served as a producer and occasional director of the series.His film credits include License to Wed (2007), Leatherheads (2008), Away We Go (2009), It's Complicated (2009), Something Borrowed (2011), Big Miracle (2012), Promised Land (2012), Aloha (2015), and 13 Hours: The Secret Soldiers of Benghazi (2016). Krasinski directed and starred in Brief Interviews with Hideous Men (2009), The Hollars (2016), and A Quiet Place (2018), the latter of which he starred in with his wife, Emily Blunt. In 2018, Krasinski was included on Time's annual list of the 100 most influential people in the world.
